# Property & Asset Verification

> Land records and collateral verification databases for mortgage and secured lending

---

## Overview

Property and asset verification is critical for BFSI sector when:
- Providing home loans/mortgages
- Taking property as collateral
- Verifying asset ownership
- Conducting due diligence on secured transactions

---

## Why BFSI Uses Property Databases

**Collateral verification** - Confirms property ownership before approving mortgages. **Title search** - Ensures no prior claims, liens, or encumbrances on the property.

---

## Detailed State Information

### Maharashtra - EGRASS/IGR

**What It Contains:**
- Property registrations
- Stamp duty records
- Encumbrance certificates
- Sale deeds
- Mortgage details

**Cost:** Free search, minimal fees for certificates

**Why Critical:** Mumbai and Pune are major real estate markets; essential for housing finance companies.

---

### Karnataka - Bhoomi

**What It Contains:**
- Land records
- RTC (Record of Rights, Tenancy and Crops)
- Mutations
- Survey numbers
- Agricultural land details

**Cost:** Free search

**Why Critical:** Bangalore is a tech hub with high real estate lending activity. Agricultural land verification for Kisan Credit Cards.

---

### Delhi - DLRC

**What It Contains:**
- Property registrations
- Encumbrance certificates
- Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) records
- Court orders on properties

**Cost:** Free search, fees for certificates

**Why Critical:** NCR (National Capital Region) is a major real estate market with high-value properties.

## National Databases

### CERSAI (Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest of India)

**What It Contains:**
- Security interests on movable and immovable assets
- Charges registered by banks and financial institutions
- Vehicle loans
- Machinery and equipment loans
- Stock/inventory financing

**Direct Access:**
| Resource | URL | Cost |
|----------|-----|------|
| **Public Search** | https://www.cersai.org.in/CERSAI/searchRequestAction.do | FREE |

**Why BFSI Uses This:**
**Priority of charge** - Determines which lender gets paid first in case of default. **Prevents double financing** - Checks if asset is already pledged to another lender.

## How Property Verification Works

### Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Identify Property**
- Get property address
- Survey number or plot number
- Owner details

**Step 2: Search Land Records**
- Go to respective state portal
- Search by survey number or owner name
- Download property card/RTC

**Step 3: Check Encumbrances**
- Order encumbrance certificate (EC)
- Shows all transactions in last 13-30 years
- Identifies mortgages, sales, disputes

**Step 4: CERSAI Search**
- Search if property has registered charges
- Check for existing loans against the property

**Step 5: Physical Verification**
- Site visit by bank's technical team
- Verify property boundaries
- Check for unauthorized construction

**Step 6: Legal Opinion**
- Bank's legal team reviews all documents
- Title verification
- Clearance for mortgage creation

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Encumbrance Certificate (EC)** | Document showing all transactions on a property |
| **Mutation** | Transfer of property ownership in government records |
| **Survey Number** | Unique identifier for land parcels |
| **RTC** | Record of Rights, Tenancy and Crops |
| **Khata** | Property tax assessment record |
| **Patta** | Land ownership document |
| **7/12 Extract** | Maharashtra land records document |

## Red Flags for BFSI

⚠️ **Warning Signs:**
- Multiple encumbrances
- Pending court cases
- Agricultural land being sold as residential (illegal in some states)
- Survey number not found in records
- Owner name mismatch
- Disputed boundaries
- Unauthorized construction

## Use Cases

### Home Loans
1. Verify property ownership
2. Check encumbrances
3. Ensure clear title
4. Create mortgage in bank's favor

### Loan Against Property (LAP)
1. Current market value assessment
2. Existing loan verification via CERSAI
3. Title clearance
4. Second charge creation

### Builder Financing
1. Verify land ownership
2. Check approvals (building plans, occupancy certificate)
3. Encumbrance clearance on land
4. Project-specific searches

### Agricultural Loans
1. Land records from Bhoomi/state portals
2. Verify cultivator/owner
3. Land area confirmation
4. Crop details for loan sizing

## Best Practices for BFSI

### Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Property card/RTC from state portal
- [ ] Encumbrance certificate (13-30 years)
- [ ] Sale deed copies
- [ ] Approved building plans
- [ ] Khata/property tax receipts
- [ ] CERSAI search report
- [ ] Legal opinion
- [ ] Technical valuation report

### Verification Timeline
- **Land records search:** 1-2 days
- **Encumbrance certificate:** 3-7 days (varies by state)
- **CERSAI search:** Same day
- **Legal opinion:** 7-15 days
- **Technical valuation:** 3-7 days
